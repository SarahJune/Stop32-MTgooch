In the beginning..

Like all good things STOP32 was the brain child of two young, enterprising youths who, at the time, were flexing their cerebral spaces while attending university amongst the trees at Whitworth University in Eastern Washington State.

These two entrepreneurs, who shared a common passion in the arts of all things photography, decided to do a service to their community and a revolutionary club where photographers of all ages, skills, and experiences could come together and bond.

Behind the name : STOP32

In photography, an aperture or f/stop controls the amount of light entering into the photograph as well as the depth of field. Embarking on a mission of embracing photographers from all walks of life, our esteemed co-founders wanted the name of the club to incorporate this welcoming spirit. In photography the larger the fstop number, the greater the depth of field. For example, an fstop of 32 is often used for landscapes or when a photographer wants a wide depth of field in the photo. As such they settled upon 32 to reflect this desire to invite photographers from all skill levels, and experiences.

The name also pays homage to a significant icon of photography from the early 1900's. In the early 1930's some of the worlds most renowned photographers came together, much like our co-founders, and formed "Group F/64". This group included such massive names such Ansel Adams, Edward Weston, and Imogen Cunningham. These revolutionaries were some of the greatest names of the time and were focused on returning photography to simplicity and art with careful compositions and perfect exposure.

Life beyond inception..

Alas, while the on campus club has since perished, the life blood of this idea is manifested and revived through the internet. While its original members and founders are spread throughout the US, this online medium provides us a source to re-token the past and rekindle the STOP32 community of photographers.
